Biscuits

Description

This dish is a southern classic. It's my go to when I want to use up the buttermilk I purchased to make these earlier in the week. A wonderfully flakey quickbread perfect for any time of day.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 5 tablespoons cold butter, cut into 1/2-inch slices
  • 3/4 cup and 2 tablespoons buttermilk or yogurt

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 450Â°F. Mix togeter the dry ingredients in a large bowl. Add the butter and rub it into the flour using your thumbs against your other fingers. (Like you're gesturing about money)
  2. Add the buttermilk and stir until the dough comes together and forms a ball. On a clean workspace spread flour and turn out your dough onto the workspace. Knead the dough a few times, folding it over, using the heel of your hand to push it away, giving it a quarter turn, and repeating a few times.
  3. Press out the dough to 3/4 inches of thickness and cut out 1 1/2 - 2 1/2 inch rounds, ideally with a biscuit or cookie cutter, but in a pinch a drinking glass works. But the rounds onto a baking sheet, press the scraps over dough back together, cut out more biscuits and repeat if possible.
  4. Bake for 5 - 10 minutes, depending on the size and how they're coming along. When the biscuits are golden brown transfer them onto a wire rack and serve quickly. Although, I've found they sometimes benefit from about 3 - 5 minutes of rest.
